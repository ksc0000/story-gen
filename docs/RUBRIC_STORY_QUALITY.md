# Story Quality Rubric

## Purpose
The Story Quality rubric evaluates the narrative structure, age-appropriateness, and emotional impact of the generated picture book. The goal is to ensure the story is engaging, coherent, and suitable for the target audience.

## Scoring Criteria (1-5)
The overall Story Quality score is assigned based on the following levels:

| Score | Rating | Description |
|---|---|---|
| 5 | Strong Product Quality | Excellent narrative, perfectly suited for the target age, and provides a deep sense of satisfaction. (商品品質として強い) |
| 4 | Generally Good | Solid story structure with minor issues that do not detract from the overall experience. (概ね良い) |
| 3 | Readable / Improvement Needed | The story is understandable but lacks depth, has pacing issues, or minor logical gaps. (読めるが改善余地あり) |
| 2 | Insufficient Quality | Significant issues with coherence, engagement, or age-appropriateness. (品質不足) |
| 1 | Failure | The story is incoherent, inappropriate, or fails to form a narrative. (失敗) |

## Evaluation Axes
Reviewers should consider the following axes when determining the score:

- **Story Coherence**: Are the plot points logically connected? Is there a clear beginning, middle, and end?
- **Age Appropriateness**: Is the vocabulary, sentence length, and complexity suitable for the target age group?
- **Emotional Satisfaction**: Does the story evoke the intended emotions (joy, curiosity, etc.) and provide a satisfying conclusion?
- **Page Length Balance**: Is the amount of text per page consistent and appropriate for the pacing?
- **Ending Satisfaction**: Does the story conclude naturally without feeling abrupt?

## Examples

### Good (Score 4-5)
- **Coherent**: A clear journey where the character faces a challenge and overcomes it.
- **Engaging**: Uses descriptive but age-appropriate language that captures a child's imagination.
- **Pacing**: Each page builds on the last, maintaining interest until the final page.

### Poor (Score 1-2)
- **Logical Jumps**: Characters suddenly change locations or situations without explanation.
- **Dry/Mechanical**: The text reads like a set of instructions rather than a story.
- **Inappropriate**: Using complex abstract concepts or vocabulary for a 3-year-old.

## Nuances
- **Target Age**: A "Good" story for a 7-year-old might be "Poor" for a 2-year-old due to complexity.
- **Pacing**: Ensure the climax and resolution are given enough space rather than being rushed in the last sentence.
