# Story Goal Adherence Rubric

## Purpose
The Story Goal Adherence rubric evaluates how well the generated story follows and achieves the `storyGoal` and `mainQuestObject` defined at the start of the creation process. This ensures narrative focus and prevents the AI from drifting into unrelated or contradictory plotlines.

## Scoring Criteria (1-5)
The Story Goal Adherence score is assigned based on the following levels:

| Score | Rating | Description |
|---|---|---|
| 5 | Perfect Adherence | The `storyGoal` is clearly established, consistently pursued on every page, and satisfyingly resolved in the ending. |
| 4 | Strong Adherence | The goal is clear and followed throughout the narrative. Minor pacing issues or slightly weak page-to-page transitions exist but do not detract from the main arc. |
| 3 | Moderate Adherence | The goal is present but the narrative occasionally drifts or the resolution feels slightly weak or abrupt. The connection between pages and the goal could be stronger. |
| 2 | Weak Adherence | Significant drift from the `storyGoal`. The goal is only superficially mentioned and does not drive the narrative actions. |
| 1 | Failure | The `storyGoal` is ignored, forgotten, or contradicted by the story text. |

## Evaluation Axes
Reviewers should assess adherence across these key axes:

1.  **Goal Clarity (Opening)**: Is the `storyGoal` or a clear hook leading to it introduced in the opening page?
2.  **Narrative Continuity**: Do the actions and events on each page logically progress the story toward the `storyGoal`?
3.  **Main Quest Object Integration**: Is the `mainQuestObject` (if defined) used effectively as a central element in the pursuit of the goal?
4.  **Drift & Noise Prevention**: Are `forbiddenQuestObjects` successfully excluded from the main plot? Do `hiddenDetails` remain secondary rather than hijacking the story?
5.  **Resolution Satisfaction (Ending)**: Is the `storyGoal` explicitly achieved or addressed in the final pages? Does the ending provide closure related to the initial goal?

## Examples

### Good (Score 4-5)
- **Focused Arc**: If the goal is "Find the lost star," the opening shows the star falling, the middle pages show the search in different locations, and the ending shows the star being found and returned.
- **Object Significance**: The `mainQuestObject` is the key to solving the problem or the primary thing the character interacts with.

### Poor (Score 1-2)
- **Goal Drift**: The goal is "Find the lost star," but by page 2, the character is suddenly focused on having a picnic and never mentions the star again.
- **Object Replacement**: A `forbiddenQuestObject` (e.g., "don't use a map") becomes the primary way the character finds their way.
- **Abrupt Resolution**: The character is searching for the star on page 3, and on page 4, it's suddenly morning and they are home, with no mention of whether the star was found.

## Nuances
- **Sub-plots**: Brief detours are acceptable if they ultimately serve the `storyGoal` (e.g., asking a friend for directions).
- **Implicit vs. Explicit**: For younger children (Age 2-4), the adherence should be very explicit. For older children (Age 7+), more subtle narrative connections are acceptable as long as the arc remains coherent.
