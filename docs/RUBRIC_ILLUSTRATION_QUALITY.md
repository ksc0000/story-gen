# Illustration Quality Rubric

## Purpose
The Illustration Quality rubric assesses the visual appeal, adherence to prompts, and technical execution of the generated images. The goal is to provide a consistent and high-quality visual experience throughout the book.

## Scoring Criteria (1-5)
The overall Illustration Quality score is assigned based on the following levels:

| Score | Rating | Description |
|---|---|---|
| 5 | Excellent Quality | High consistency, great composition, and perfectly reflects the style. (一貫性・構図・style が良い) |
| 4 | Good Quality | Solid illustrations with only minor visual jitters. (軽微な揺れのみ) |
| 3 | Acceptable Quality | Generally fine but with some noticeable discomfort or inconsistencies. (一部違和感) |
| 2 | Low Quality | Conspicuous collapse in quality or significant inconsistencies across pages. (崩れや不一致が目立つ) |
| 1 | Breakdown | Significant artifacts, technical failures, or major visual breakdowns. (破綻) |

## Evaluation Axes
Reviewers should consider the following axes when determining the score:

- **Visual Consistency**: Do the illustrations maintain the same art style, color palette, and world-building across all pages?
- **Scene Relevance**: Do the images accurately reflect the actions and emotions described in the text?
- **Style Consistency**: Does the illustration adhere to the selected `styleBible` or target style?
- **Artifact Avoidance**:
    - **No Text Artifacts**: Avoidance of signs, posters, banners, labels, or storefront signs with nonsensical text.
    - **Technical Correctness**: Absence of distorted limbs, faces, or impossible physical objects.
- **Prompt Completeness**: Does the illustration capture all the key elements requested in the prompt (characters, background, mood)?

## Examples

### High Quality (Score 4-5)
- **Style Adherence**: The art style remains identical from the cover to the last page.
- **Dynamic Composition**: Engaging use of perspectives (e.g., close-ups for emotion, wide shots for setting).
- **Clean Images**: No visible AI artifacts or nonsensical text.

### Low Quality (Score 1-2)
- **Style Drift**: The character's art style changes from "watercolor" on page 1 to "3D render" on page 2.
- **Major Artifacts**: Characters with extra fingers or floating objects.
- **Text Blobs**: Unwanted signs or labels appearing in the background.

## Nuances
- **Complexity**: More complex scenes (many characters, detailed backgrounds) are harder to keep consistent and should be judged accordingly.
- **Mood**: The lighting and color should shift naturally with the story's mood (e.g., brighter for joy, softer for bedtime).
